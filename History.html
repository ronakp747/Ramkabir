History of NJ Bhagat Samaj

In 1972, Dr. I.T. Bhagat and several families in the Edison area of New Jersey began celebrating three key festivals—Ramnavami, Janmashtami, and Diwali—at Dr. I.T. Bhagat’s home. They gathered to share food, sing Bhajans, and engage in spiritual discussions.

By 2007, some Bhagat families initiated bi-weekly Samagam Satsang meetings. During this period, Swami Jagdish Chandra Maharaj visited New Jersey, inspiring greater community involvement. This led to the first Bhagat Samaj picnic in 2009, which further encouraged more family participation.

After the COVID-19 pandemic, activities shifted online, but in September 2022, the New Jersey Bhagat Samaj organization was officially established. The organization expanded its reach, including families from Pennsylvania, Maryland, Connecticut, New York, and New Jersey.

Milestones

  1. 1972

    Festivals (Ramnavami, Janmashtami, Diwali) celebrated at Dr. I.T. Bhagat’s home; Bhajans, food, and spiritual discussions.

  2. 2007

    Bi-weekly Samagam Satsang meetings begin; visit by Swami Jagdish Chandra Maharaj inspires wider participation.

  3. 2009

    First Bhagat Samaj picnic held, encouraging further family involvement.

  4. 2020–2021

    Activities move online during the COVID-19 pandemic.

  5. September 2022

    New Jersey Bhagat Samaj officially established; outreach expands to PA, MD, CT, NY, and NJ.

Major Annual Events

  • Ramnavami
  • Janmashtami
  • Summer Picnic
  • Diwali

These events are celebrated on a larger scale, featuring enhanced spiritual and cultural activities. The organization also conducts weekly youth spiritual and cultural learning sessions.
